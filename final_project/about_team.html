Our Team

Imagine...take earth, create a form, and by applying heat and liquid glass, and you can forever change it into something that can bring joy- feels like magic!

Ann Founder

Ann is one of the visionary founders of our pottery studio. Her love for pottery began at a young age and has grown into a lifelong passion. Ann delights in sharing the transformative joy of pottery with everyone—encouraging both seasoned creators and curious beginners to get their hands a little muddy and experience the art for themselves. Her dedication and infectious enthusiasm continue to inspire our community to explore their creativity and find fulfillment in every crafted piece.

Dave Founder

Dave is not only a founder of our pottery studio but also a seasoned expert in his field. His global journeys have taken him around the world to master diverse techniques from various cultures, with a particular focus on Chinese tea cups. Dave spent years honing his craft, studying traditional methods passed down through generations. He learned from renowned master Jiang Feier, perfecting the art of Zisha teapot making, and dedicated significant time to immersing himself in the ancient practices of Yixing clay teapot making—a tradition that dates back to the 16th century. His expertise and passion bring an unmatched depth and authenticity to our studio, inspiring every piece crafted in his honor.

Sophy Event Manager

Sophy is a talented pottery artist whose passion for clay and creativity shines through every unique, hand-crafted piece she creates. With a keen eye for detail and a deep appreciation for organic shapes, she masterfully blends traditional techniques with modern design elements. As our lead event manager, Sophy curates inspiring exhibits that invite people to see the beauty she sees in clay, showcasing stunning works from creators around the globe. Her events are a celebration of artistry and a wonderful opportunity to experience the transformative power of pottery firsthand.

Martin

Martin is a talented pottery artist whose passion for clay radiates in every unique, hand-crafted piece he creates. Watching him work is like witnessing a meditative dance of precision and creativity—each subtle, deliberate movement transforms raw clay into organic masterpieces that speak to his refined artistry. Martin specializes in Kyusu tea pots, a tradition dating all the way back to the Song Dynasty (960–1279). He spent 10 years living in Nagasaki, Japan, mastering this technique and now teaches our advanced classes, inspiring others to explore the rich history and artistry of pottery.

Jill

Jill graduated at the top of her class from Alfred University's New York State College of Ceramics, where she developed a fearless approach to art. Always experimenting and merging diverse styles and techniques, she creates pieces that are truly one-of-a-kind, pushing the boundaries of traditional ceramics by blending classic methods with modern innovations. Her dynamic and visually captivating creations inspire both peers and emerging artists to explore their own unique artistic visions. In addition to her creative pursuits, Jill passionately shares her expertise by teaching intermediate and advanced classes, nurturing the next generation of ceramic artists.
